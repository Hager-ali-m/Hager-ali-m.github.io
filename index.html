01 / About

Bridging technology and human experience in urban mobility

I am a doctoral researcher at the University of Münster, working within the Marie Curie SCALA project on spatial communication across the lifespan. My work sits at the intersection of geospatial science, urban planning, and cognitive research.

My PhD investigates how navigation systems can better support bicyclists by accounting for perceived and computed bikeability, with particular attention to how age and gender influence cycling comfort and route choice.

Beyond my doctoral research, I lead the ABC program, coordinating university partnerships and institutional collaborations across multiple countries.

03 / Projects

MSc Thesis

Assessing Compliance with the X-Minute City Concept: A Bikeability and Accessibility Approach in Münster

Supervised by Chris Kray, Simge Özdal Oktay & Ana Cristina Costa — University of Münster

Developed a comprehensive framework for evaluating urban compliance with the x-minute city concept through the lens of cycling. The study combined cumulative accessibility measurement with a multi-domain bikeability index (infrastructure, safety, comfort, environment), weighted via Analytic Hierarchy Process with input from domain experts including Naviki and city traffic planners.

Bikeability domains: infrastructure, safety, comfort, environment

Spatial inequalities were analyzed using the Gini index and spatial autocorrelation (Global and Local Moran's I), alongside a sufficiency criteria model. An interactive digital tool was built to support data-driven decision-making for policymakers and residents.
